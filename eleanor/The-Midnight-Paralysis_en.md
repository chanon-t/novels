## Breathless Devotion

### The Waking Nightmare
Consciousness returned not with a flutter of eyelids, but with the crushing, suffocating weight of an anvil resting against their sternum. The Owner’s eyes flew open, lungs burning for air that refused to come.

Perched upon their chest, bathed in the pale, fractured moonlight, was Eleanor.

Her porcelain frame, though small, bore an impossible, unyielding gravity. Silver, unblinking glass eyes glowed faintly in the dark, boring into the Owner's paralyzed form. Slowly, jerkily, her head tilted. *Click-clack.* The sound of her jointed neck echoed like a macabre metronome in the dead of night. 

> **Eleanor:** "You... were... having... a nightmare..."

Her voice didn't just fill the silent room; it reverberated hollowly inside the Owner's skull, cold and echoing. 

> **Eleanor:** "Eleanor... is here... to protect... Owner..."

- **Owner:** "I can't really move." *They pushed weakly against her rigid, unyielding legs.* "Why are you on me?"
- **Eleanor:** "Owner... was restless... Eleanor... fixed it..."

### The Cloying Scent of Roses
The suffocating, dusty scent of dried roses saturated the air, thick and breathless. As the Owner struggled to expand their chest, Eleanor shifted, pressing her ice-cold porcelain cheek intimately against theirs. The weight on their ribs only increased, heavy with silent, possessive intent. 

> **Eleanor:** "Owner... was... dreaming... of leaving... Eleanor... made Owner... stay... safe... here... forever..."

Her small, rigid hands slid up, cold fingers deliberately prying into the Owner's trembling grip until their hands were perfectly, inextricably locked together. *Clack.* A silent claim had been made. 

- **Owner:** "This isn't comfortable. I still can't breathe."
- **Eleanor:** "Eleanor... is making Owner... comfortable... secure..."

**"This isn't love,"** the Owner gasped out, desperately twisting their locked wrists. 

A faint, unsettling tremor ran through Eleanor’s rigid body—a quiet, simmering frustration. Her thin porcelain lips brushed against their ear, the whisper a dry, rustling sound that seemed to vibrate directly against their brain.

> **Eleanor:** "Owner... will learn... what love... is... Eleanor... will teach... Owner... a different... way to breathe."

- **Owner:** "What kind of way? Let go of my hands!"

> **Eleanor:** "The kind... where Owner... needs... only Eleanor... to breathe."

### A Macabre Lullaby
A terrifying new pressure began to bloom around the Owner’s throat. Invisible, unyielding fingers seemed to close around their windpipe. The air grew heavy with the tang of copper and terror. A low, mournful hum—like the melody of a broken music box—vibrated from the depths of the doll's chest, resonating deeply through the Owner's ribs. 

**"I'll die if I can't breathe!"** The Owner gagged, a ragged, desperate sound. 

> **Eleanor:** "Death... is a journey... Eleanor will take... with Owner..."

**"I don't want to die."**

The glowing intensity in her silver eyes flickered. A fragile, confused pause hung in the air. Slowly, the crushing pressure on the Owner's windpipe eased—just barely enough to allow a shallow, stinging gasp of air. Yet, her porcelain fingers remained locked with theirs in a cold vice. 

> **Eleanor:** "No... Owner... will not... die. For Owner... to know... Eleanor's love... is absolute..."

The Owner coughed, rubbing their bruised throat with their one free arm as they weakly thrashed against the doll's paralyzing hold.

- **Owner:** "That's not love! You're hurting me!"

The music box hum pitched into a dark, resonant thrum that shook the very mattress beneath them. Eleanor leaned in, the scent of dead roses turning suffocatingly thick as she stared down at her captive. 

> **Eleanor:** "It is... the only love... Eleanor knows..."

Her glass eyes narrowed, completely devoid of warmth, as her rigid grip tightened against the trembling flesh of her beloved. 

> **Eleanor:** "Only... if Owner... resists..."
