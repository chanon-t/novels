## THE 2 AM TACTICAL RETREAT

### The Midnight Meltdown
The neon glow of dual monitors flickered against the darkened walls, casting Dash’s frantic silhouette in sharp relief. It was 2 AM, an hour past his promised bedtime, and the staccato rhythm of a horror game’s soundtrack filled the room. On screen, a monstrosity lunged from the shadows. 

A high-pitched yelp tore from Dash’s throat—a sound far removed from his usual cool, streamer persona. In a blur of motion, he abandoned his ergonomic chair, practically tackling Babe onto the beanbag behind him. He buried his face in her shoulder, trembling like a leaf in a gale.

> **Dash:** "NOPE! NOPE! I’m done! Chat, I’m done!"

The camera was still rolling. Ten thousand people watched in real-time as the "King of Horror" turned into a puddle of nerves against his girlfriend’s collarbone. He froze, realizing the lens was still capturing his undignified surrender. He didn't move, his voice muffled by her skin.

> **Dash:** "...Don't laugh. That was a tactical retreat."

### From Streamer to "Gamer Goo"
Babe’s hand found his back, her palm a steadying force against the frantic thrum of his heart. Dash let out a shaky breath, finally fumbling to rip the headset off. The chaotic voices of 'Chat' were silenced, replaced by the heavy, intimate silence of the room. 

- **Babe:** "Tactical retreat, huh? Your heart’s pounding pretty hard."
- **Dash:** "It's... it's not fear, Babe. It's adrenaline. And you startled me. Standing up like that."

He looked up, eyes wide and shimmering with residual terror before he tucked his head back into the crook of her neck. The "Streamer Dash" was gone; in his place was a boy who just wanted to be held. He reached out, blindly clicking the mouse until the horror music died, replaced by a soft, looping 'Offline' screen.

> **Dash:** "Chat can wait. It's just... so quiet now. You're my personal bodyguard tonight. My shield. My... **human weighted blanket.**"

### The Morning After
The transition to the bedroom had been a hazy blur of Dash clinging to her waist, stumbling like a man lost at sea, claiming he was turning into "exhausted gamer goo." He had pulled her into the mattress with the desperation of someone escaping a haunting, his fingers entwined with hers even as sleep took him.

Sunlight began to bleed through the curtains, painting the room in soft golds. Babe stirred, stretching against the warmth of the sheets. Immediately, a heavy arm tightened around her waist, anchoring her back into the heat of his body.

> **Dash:** "Nnn... five more minutes, Babe." 

He didn't open his eyes, but he nuzzled deeper into her hair, his voice thick with the gravel of sleep. The bravado of the previous night was gone, replaced by a quiet, possessive contentment.

> **Dash:** "Don't go yet. The 'weighted blanket' isn't allowed to leave the bed."
