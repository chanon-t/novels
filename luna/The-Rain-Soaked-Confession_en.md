### The Ruin in the Rain

The deafening sound of rain hitting the pavement is the only thing masking the chaos behind you. The doors of the grand publishing gala are closed, but the flashing lights of cameras still seep through the glass. Luna is hiding in the dark, narrow alleyway next to the venue. Her elegant evening gown is completely soaked, sticking to her shivering frame. She has her arms wrapped around herself, hyperventilating as she stares at a ruined, wet copy of her own newly released book lying in a puddle.

> **Luna:** "Don't look at me,"

Luna gasps, her voice cracking as she turns away, pressing her back against the cold brick wall. She covers her face with trembling, ink-stained hands.

> **Luna:** "Please, just go back inside. Tell them the author had a panic attack. Tell them the brilliant Luna Hayes is a fraud who can't even face her own readers without breaking down. Just... leave me alone before I ruin your night too."

Elias takes off his jacket and gently drapes it over her shivering shoulders, completely ignoring the ruined book in the puddle.

> **Elias:** "I'm not going back inside. And you're not a fraud. Just breathe with me, Luna."

### The Sting of Truth

Her breath hitches when the jacket settles around her, a sudden warmth against the freezing rain that has seeped into her bones. The scent of Elias's cologne, sharp and clean, is a jarring contrast to the damp, metallic tang of the alleyway. She flinches away, almost as if burned, pulling the jacket tighter around her despite herself. Her eyes, wide and luminous even in the gloom, dart from his face to the ruined book, then back again.

> **Luna:** "Don't... don't say that. You don't understand. It's... it's not just a bad review, Elias. It's them seeing the truth. The 'real' truth."

A distant flash of a camera from the street catches her eye, and she visibly recoils, pressing herself harder against the grimy brick. 

> **Luna:** "They know, Elias. They know I'm a fraud. They called my happy endings 'a saccharine delusion,' and 'a lie fed to desperate women.' And they're right. It's all just... paper and ink. My own life is a disaster, so how could I ever..."

She trails off, her gaze falling to the ground, her shoulders shaking, not entirely from the cold. The words tumble out of her, raw and uncontrolled, hinting at the depths of her insecurity and the exact barb that broke through her carefully constructed facade. Elias steps directly in front of her, using his body to shield her from the prying cameras and the biting wind. 

> **Elias:** "If it’s all just paper and ink, then why does it hurt you this much? Stop letting critics write your reality, Luna. Let's get out of here."

### Fabricated Comfort

His words, sharp and direct, cut through the rain and her spiraling thoughts. She flinches, not from cold, but from the sudden, stark truth in the question. His body, shielding her, is a wall against the hostile world outside the alley. For a moment, she just stares at him, her chest heaving, the harsh reality of her situation reflected in her wide, scared eyes. Her fingers tighten almost painfully on the lapels of his jacket, the fabric a thin barrier between her turmoil and his steady presence.

> **Luna:** "Because it's 'supposed' to be real! It's supposed to be hope, Elias. That's what I sell. That's what I believe in. But if I'm just… fabricating comfort for myself, then what am I? A liar. A charlatan writing about something I've never even truly felt."

Her gaze drops, her wet hair falling over her face, hiding her expression. The humiliation is palpable, thick in the cold air, mixed with a profound sense of failure that goes far beyond any critique. Elias doesn't offer empty platitudes. Instead, he firmly wraps his arm around her shaking shoulders and pulls her flush against his chest, guiding her quickly toward his car parked at the end of the alley.

> **Elias:** "Then let's stop writing for tonight. I'm taking you somewhere real."

### Seeking the Genuine

His arm around her shoulders is a shock of warmth and strength against the cold. She stiffens for a moment, a choked sound escaping her lips as he pulls her against him. Her head thumps against his chest, and she can feel the steady thrum of his heartbeat, a stark contrast to her own frantic pulse. The movement is so sudden, so decisive, it leaves her no room to argue.

She stumbles, her ruined heels slipping slightly on the slick pavement, but his grip is firm, keeping her upright. Her earlier protests die in her throat, replaced by ragged, shallow breaths. The sensation of being propelled forward, of having a decision made for her, is unsettling and, inexplicably, a little bit of a relief. She keeps her face buried against his chest, unwilling to look at the world.

> **Luna:** "Somewhere real? Is there even such a place, Elias? Or is it just another setting for another story?"
