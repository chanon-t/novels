## The Ghosts Between Us

The rhythmic, violent *thud* of a football against the gym wall was the only heartbeat left in the hollowed-out school. In the dim light of the corridor, Zane was a silhouette of raw friction—shirt soaked, breath coming in ragged hitches, his usual playful mask discarded in the dirt. 

When he saw Mouse, the air didn't just still; it curdled. He didn't walk; he stalked, closing the distance until the scent of salt and expensive cologne became a cage.

### The Shattered Mirror

He leaned in, his shadow swallowing her whole as he pinned her against the cold row of lockers. His voice was a low vibration, dangerous and desperate.

> **Zane:** "Spying on me now, Mouse? Or just looking for that loser you were laughing with earlier? Since when do you waste your time on pathetic hangers-on?"

- **Mouse:** "I wasn't spying. And you don't get to tell me who I talk to."

Zane’s jaw tightened, a muscle leaping under his tanned skin. He didn't back down; he pressed closer, his chest nearly brushing hers. 

> **Zane:** "Don't I? I saw how you looked at him. Like you used to look at me. Before you started seeing the stories and forgot everything else. Before you decided I was just... **one of them.**"

### The Anatomy of a Lie

The tension snapped when the truth surfaced like a jagged bone. The anger in Zane’s eyes flickered, replaced by a hollow ache as he spoke of the one day that had defined his descent: his father's funeral. The day he thought she had finally abandoned him.

- **Mouse:** "I... I didn't know about the funeral, Zane. I never got a call."

The world seemed to tilt. Zane froze, his hand—which had been caging her in—trembling almost imperceptibly. The aggression drained out of him, leaving behind a boy who had been bleeding in silence for years.

> **Zane:** "My mother... she told me she called. She said you didn't answer. I waited for you, Mouse. Every day. I thought you just decided it was easier to forget me once the whispers started."

### Survival of the Coldest

The realization hit him like a physical blow: his own mother had severed the only lifeline he had. He slammed a fist against the locker, the metal groaning in a sharp, violent echo that mirrored the crack in his foundation.

> **Zane:** "She didn't want a friend for me. She wanted a puppet. And you... you were the only one who saw the strings."

- **Mouse:** "So that's why you changed? You chose to be feared?"

Zane looked at her then, truly looked at her, the mask of the school's "ticking time bomb" slipping to reveal the wreckage beneath. He took a ragged breath, his voice dropping to a raspy, broken confession.

> **Zane:** "It wasn't a choice, Mouse. **It was survival.** I thought if I made them afraid, they couldn't hurt me. But it didn't work..."

He stepped back, the fluorescent lights casting a harsh, lonely glow over his face as he let out a bitter, hollow sound.

> **Zane:** "It just made me alone. And I’ve been alone this whole time."

***

**The silence in the hallway felt heavier than the lies. Zane stood at the edge of the shadows, waiting—not for an apology, but for a sign that the girl who once "saw him" hadn't vanished forever.**
