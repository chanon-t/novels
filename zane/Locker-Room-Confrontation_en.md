## The Echo of a Broken Claim

The school hallways didn't just feel empty; they felt haunted. The rhythmic, violent *thud* of a football hitting the gym wall echoed like a heartbeat through the silence. Inside, Zane was a storm of sweat and aggression, his usual golden-boy facade stripped away to reveal something far more dangerous.

### The Trapping
When he caught sight of her, the rhythm stopped. He didn't just walk; he stalked, closing the distance until the scent of salt and heat overwhelmed the sterile air of the lockers. 

> **Zane:** "Spying on me now, Mouse?"

He leaned in, his shadow swallowing her whole as he pinned her against the cold metal. His breath ghosted across her forehead, heavy with the metallic tang of exertion.

- **Mouse:** "I wasn't spying. And why are you in my face right now?"
- **Zane:** "Because I *can*, Mouse. And because you’re mine. You always have been."

His thumb brushed against the inside of her wrist—a feather-light touch that hummed with possessive electricity. 

### The Weight of "Kid Promises"
The air between them fractured when she tried to pull away. The arrogant smirk on Zane's face didn't just fade; it shattered. For a fleeting second, the terrifying athlete was gone, replaced by a boy holding onto a memory that was clearly killing him.

> **Zane:** "You think this is insane? After everything? What about the treehouse, Mouse? What about those promises we made under the old oak, etched into the wood?"

**"Those were kid promises, Zane,"** she countered, her voice a blade that cut through his desperation. 

He flinched as if she’d physically struck him. The grip on her arm tightened instinctively, his head bowing as a raw, aching vulnerability bled through his tough exterior. When he looked up, a single tear traced a path through the salt on his cheek.

> **Zane:** "Kid promises? Is that what you call it? I remember every detail, Mouse. Every touch. Every word. And I’m trying to figure out why you pretend I don't even exist."

### The Cold Departure
The rejection was the final blow. The fire in his eyes didn't just go out; it turned to ice. He stepped back abruptly, the sudden chasm of space between them feeling more violent than the confrontation itself. The shaking in his hands stopped, replaced by a terrifying, hollow stillness.

- **Zane:** "You're right. I'm crazy. Always have been... especially for you."

He turned on his heel, his footsteps echoing with a finality that made the hallway feel unnaturally vast. He left her standing in the silence, the faint scent of his spice-and-cologne lingering like a ghost. 

But as the sound of his departure faded, something caught the light on the dark floor tiles—a small, dark object left behind in the wake of his storm.
